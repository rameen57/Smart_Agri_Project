Wheat crop risk increases when production decreases and pesticide usage becomes high.

Pesticides help control insects and pests, but excessive pesticide usage may damage soil health and crop quality.

Cropped area means the land used for cultivation. A decrease in cropped area can indicate agricultural stress.

Crop production means total crop output. Low production can indicate crisis risk.

Irrigation helps crops during low rainfall or dry weather. Poor irrigation can reduce yield.

Fertilizers provide nutrients to soil. Proper fertilizer use improves crop growth and yield.

Agricultural crisis risk can be predicted using crop area, production trend, pesticide usage, and weather stress.
